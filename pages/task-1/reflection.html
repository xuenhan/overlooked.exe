TASK 1: Finding Information

WHAT YOU DID:

You located an email address under two different visual conditions.

WHAT CHANGED?

The structure and content of the page remained the same. Only the visual presentation was altered.

Baseline Experience Barrier Experience
Clear headings with strong visual hierarchy Headings no longer visually distinct
High colour contrast Lower colour contrast (text harder to read)
Email address labelled, coloured & underlined Email address blended into surrounding text (no underline)
Easy to scan and locate information Requires increased effort to scan/locate information

WHO MIGHT BE AFFECTED:

  • Users with low vision
  • Users with visual processing difficulties
  • Users experiencing eye strain or fatigue
  • Users viewing content on small screens
  • Anyone in low-light or glare conditions

CONSIDER YOUR OWN EXPERIENCE:

  • Did it take longer to find the email the second time?
  • Did you reread sections more carefully?
  • Did you feel less confident in your selection?
  • Did the page feel more overwhelming or cluttered?

WHY THIS MATTERS?

Access to information is shaped by design decisions.

The information did not change, only its presentation did.

By reducing contrast, hierarchy, and clarity, the task became more difficult. This demonstrates how accessibility barriers are often created through desing choices, not missing contents.

Small visual decisions can determine whether someone can access information independently, or at all.
